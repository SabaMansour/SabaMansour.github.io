Projects

Flow simulation over an asymetric Jakowski airfoil

Mar. 2021 – Jul. 2021

Instructor: Dr. A. Jalali

The flow around a lifting airfoil is a fundamental Fluid Mechanics phenomenon. The study of lift and drag forces, streamlines, stagnation points, and pressure coefficients related to an airfoil is an essential part of understanding this phenomenon. In this project analytic and numerical investigations was carried out respectively based on ideal flow theory (using MATLAB) and inviscid/viscid incompressible flow (using ANSYS Fluent). The results were compared to assess the accuracy of ideal flow theory assumptions for different angles of attack.


Computation and comparison of Life Cycle Assessmnet of various types of personal vehicles

Mar. 2020 – May. 2020

Instructor: Dr. P. Ahmadi

Transportation industry was accountable for approximately 30% of total emissions produced worldwide in 2019. Life cycle assessment (LCA) is of paramount importance when it comes to comparing the capital environmental cost of personal vehicles such as green-house gas emissions. In this projects myriads of personal vehicles was examined from environmental and economic point of view for a lifespan of 14 years using GREET and GHGenius software.


Designing a zero energy building's energy and HVAC systems

Apr. 2021 – Jun. 2021

Instructor: Dr. P. Ahmadi

The heating, ventilation and air cooling (HVAC) system, which is a huge system requiring great power sources,is an inseparable part of any building's design. In this project, a complete energy system of a building was precisely designed and analyzed using TRNSYS software. All the building requirements including heating and cooling loads as well as electricity consumption were taken into account. The proposed system successfully maintained the temperature between 22 and 26 centigrades (71-79 degrees Farenheit) and the relative humidity between 20% and 40% all year long for a buiding in the city of Tabriz, Iran. In order for the proposed system to be zero-energy, the building's required electricity was supplied with a renewable energy source. For this purpose, wind turbines and photovoltaic panels were compared based on initial cost and photovoltaic panels were ultimately chosen.


Two-dimensional Incompressible Laminar Navier-Stokes Equations in C++

Nov. 2019 – Feb. 2020

Instructor: Dr. A. Jalali

A framework for solving the two-dimensional incompressible laminar Navier-Stokes equations was established from scratch in C++. A lid-driven cavity flow was solved in which the top wall of a rectangular cavity moves horizontally. A SIMPLE algorithm along with finite-volume discretization was developed in which the momentum and pressure correction equations were solved by Gauss-Seidel scheme. Collocated grid arrangement was utilized for implementation of Rhie-Chow interpolation to compute the residual of continuity equation for inner control volumes. The results were verified by solving the same problem with the equations of stream function and vorticity.


A Two-dimensional Inverse Heat Conduction Problem to Estimate the Surface Heat Flux

Oct. 2019 – Dec. 2019

Instructor: Prof. F. Kowsary

Inverse heat conduction problems (IHCPs) have numerous industrial applications. A jet flow on one side and a number of heaters on the other side are utilized to fix the surface temperature of a cubic object at a specified value. This inverse heat conduction problem was considered as an optimization problem in which the heat flux of heaters are utilized to minimize the differences between the temperature profile of the object and desired temperature profile. CFD simulations were carried out to obtain the temperature profile at each iteration, while genetic and conjugate gradient algorithms were developed from scratch for the purpose of optimization.
